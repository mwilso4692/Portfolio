Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

I have used a variety of different technologies already in this course, including html, Canva, Adobe InDesign, and Adobe Illustrator. I have used each of these programs in conjunction with thoughtful rhetoric to create good designs. The projects encompassed in the "Design Beginnings" portion of this portfolio just gave me a glimpse into possibilities the programs. Design and making is all about thoughtfully using rhetoric and technology together to create an effective design.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

In the readings from "The Non Designers Design Book," I have learned a lot about the core principles of design. The core principles are proximity, alignment, repetition, and contrast. I have had to learn how to leverage these principles in my designs, especially in the logos and the good design project. In the logos, had to carefully consider alignment, contrast, and color. In the good design project, I had to identify the usage of these principles in another design while using the principles myself.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In the workshops, I was able to receive feedback from fellow students, which was helpful because my project were never perfect on the first try. I also took inspiration from the work of others because everyone is creative in their own way.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

None of my project were perfect upon submission and at workshop. It was nice to get perspective from others on how to improve. Often during workshops, I got ideas of how to improve my project, and I made edits to my work. Especially for the A/B/C logos assignment, I saw in workshop the super creative and fun logos that other people had, so I left workshop and redid all of my logos and learned the full capabilities of Adobe and Canva.

5. Learn to present your work.

We had to semi-formally present our work for Design Fuego and UX Logo Study, and we casually presented our work to our tables for the rest of the assignments. The casual presentations were nice, low-stakes way to practice articulating how you created your design, and they helped prepare me for more formal presentations. They also helped me with later technical writing, like for the Good Design project where we had to explain different features of good design.
